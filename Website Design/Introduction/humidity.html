Summary: Latitude vs. Humidity

Oddly, humidity seems to be mostly unrelated to latitude. The expectation was that cities lying around 30 S latitude, and 30 N latitude would have lower humidities because of the rainshadow effect. Presumably, the large number of cities in the humid Indian subcontinent (which lies around 30 N latitude) drones out the desert cities in the Northern latitudes. For the southern latitudes, the wet coastal cities of Southern America and Australia, similarly drone out the inland deserts of Australia, which have little if any cities. Oddly, there are a couple of cities in the extreme polar regions where there is 0 humidity, these cities are probably located in areas termed arctic deserts.
